# Easy BUD — Full Scheme Reference

## What is Easy BUD?

Easy BUD is the simplified track of the **Dedicated Fund on Branding, Upgrading and Domestic Sales (BUD Fund)**, administered by the Hong Kong Productivity Council (HKPC) on behalf of the Trade and Industry Department.

**April 23, 2026 enhancement (2026-27 Budget):** The project funding ceiling was raised from HK$100,000 to **HK$150,000**, effective for applications submitted on or after April 23, 2026. The government contributes 25% (up to HK$37,500) and the enterprise contributes 75% (at least HK$112,500) in cash upfront.

Easy BUD is a **reimbursement-only scheme**. The government does not provide upfront funding. The enterprise funds 100% of eligible project costs during execution, then claims 25% back from HKPC after the project ends and an external auditor has accepted the Final Audited Accounts.

Processing time for a complete application is approximately **30 clear working days**.

---

## Who Qualifies

To apply for Easy BUD, an enterprise must:

- Be registered in Hong Kong as a corporation, partnership, or sole proprietorship
- Hold a valid Hong Kong Business Registration Certificate
- Have **substantive business operations in Hong Kong**, evidenced by latest audited accounts (required) PLUS one of: MPF contribution records for employees, or sales invoices/receipts
- **Not** be a listed company or a subsidiary/associated company of a listed company
- Have **100 or fewer full-time employees** (SME threshold)
- Not be a government body, public body, or statutory body

**Target market requirement:** All project activities must develop the enterprise's business in Mainland China or economies with which Hong Kong has concluded a Free Trade Agreement or Investment Promotion and Protection Agreement (FTA/IPPA). Confirmed approved economies include: Mainland China, all 10 ASEAN states (Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam), New Zealand, Chile, EFTA states (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland), Georgia, Macao, Australia, South Korea, UAE, and others. Verify the full current list at www.bud.hkpc.org before finalising the target market selection.

**Self-implementation requirement:** All funded activities must be implemented by the **Hong Kong entity itself**. Activities run by the enterprise's own entity incorporated or registered in the target market do **not** qualify. This is one of the most common rejection causes — the project must be contracted, managed, and paid for from Hong Kong.

## Funding Mechanics

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Project funding ceiling | **HK$150,000** (enhanced from HK$100,000 effective 23 April 2026) |
| Government contribution | 25% of eligible costs (up to HK$37,500) |
| Enterprise cash contribution | 75% of eligible costs (at least HK$112,500 upfront) |
| Matching ratio | 1 government : 3 enterprise |
| Audit fee cap (Activity 4.10) | Up to HK$5,000; claimable at 1:3 ratio; counts toward HK$150,000 ceiling |
| Project duration | 12 months maximum — **no extensions permitted** |
| Payment method | Reimbursement only — no initial payment to enterprise |
| Processing time | ~30 clear working days from complete application |
| Application channel | Online e-form only at www.bud.hkpc.org |
| Application frequency | **Once every 3 months** per enterprise (cooldown applies regardless of outcome) |

## Eligible Activities (Type ii — Self-Implemented Specific Measures)

Easy BUD funds only **Type ii activities**: self-implemented, market-development specific measures. The enterprise must execute these directly from Hong Kong.

### 4.1 — Advertisements in the Target Market

Paid advertising targeting consumers or businesses in the approved target market. Platform management fees are fundable for **up to 6 months**.

Eligible: WeChat Official Account advertising, Xiaohongshu (RED) sponsored posts, Baidu search ads, LINE ads in Thailand/Japan, Instagram/Facebook targeting ASEAN.

Not eligible: KOL/influencer creator fees paid to individuals; ads targeting Hong Kong consumers.

### 4.2 — Exhibition Participation in the Target Market

Participation in trade exhibitions or consumer fairs held **in** the target market. Eligible costs: booth rental, booth design and construction, transport of exhibits to venue, travel and accommodation for **Hong Kong-based staff** attending.

Note: The exhibition must physically take place in the target market. Virtual-only exhibitions require clarification with HKPC.

### 4.3 — IP Registration in the Target Market

Registration of patents, trademarks, designs, utility models, or copyrights with the official registration authority in the target market. Only official registration authority fees and **external agent fees** (patent/trademark attorneys) qualify — internal staff costs do not.

**Cumulative cap: HK$600,000 per enterprise across all BUD projects of any track (lifetime).**

### 4.4 — Testing and Certification Services

Engaging an external testing or certification body to test or certify the enterprise's products for the target market. Only external agent fees qualify.

Examples: CCC certification for Mainland China, CE marking for European FTA partners, Halal certification for ASEAN markets, Japan PSE certification.

### 4.5 — Mobile App Development or Enhancement for the Target Market

Development of a new mobile application, or significant enhancement of an existing one, specifically for users in the target market.

Frame the project clearly: "WeChat Mini Program for Mainland China retail customers" or "LINE-integrated loyalty app for Thai market."

### 4.6 — Company Website Development or Enhancement for Target Market Sales

Development of a new website or significant enhancement of an existing website to enable or improve sales to the target market. Translation-only changes or minor cosmetic updates typically do not qualify as "enhancement."

**Enhancement of an existing website is capped at HK$100,000 per application.**

Examples: New localised domain with e-commerce for Japan; new Simplified Chinese site with a Mainland-compatible payment gateway.

### 4.7 — Design and Production of Promotional Materials

Design and production of printed or digital promotional materials targeting customers in the target market. Only **external agent/production house fees** are eligible — internal design costs do not qualify.

Examples: Bilingual product catalogue for Mainland China distributors; promotional video for ASEAN social media; product photography for target-market e-commerce platform listing.

### 4.8 — Establishment of Online Sales Platform for the Target Market

Building or establishing an online sales channel (marketplace storefront, e-commerce platform presence) specifically for the target market. Platform management fees are fundable for **up to 6 months**.

Examples: Tmall or JD.com flagship store for Mainland China; Lazada or Shopee seller account for ASEAN; Rakuten storefront for Japan.

Distinction from 4.6: Activity 4.6 covers the enterprise's own website; Activity 4.8 covers third-party marketplace presence.

### 4.9 — Combination of the Above

A project may combine multiple activities from 4.1–4.8. Each activity must individually qualify, and together they must serve a **single coherent project objective** in the target market. Budget each activity as a separate line item.

### 4.10 — External Audit Fee

The fee charged by an **independent external auditor** for producing the Final Audited Accounts required for the post-completion reimbursement claim. Eligible at the 1:3 ratio; counts toward the HK$150,000 ceiling; **capped at HK$5,000**.

This is a mandatory cost if you intend to claim reimbursement. Budget it at application stage — HKPC will not add it retrospectively.

## What Kills Applications

1. **Vague activity descriptions.** "Run a marketing campaign in China" is rejected. "Execute three Xiaohongshu sponsored-post campaigns in Q3 2026 targeting Shanghai female consumers aged 25–35, generating 50,000 impressions and 500 profile visits, managed by [Agency Name] at HK$12,000/month management fee" passes. Every activity needs specific deliverables, measurable KPIs, and a timeline.

2. **Activities implemented from the target market.** If the enterprise has an entity in Mainland China and that entity runs the WeChat ads — this fails. The Hong Kong entity must contract, manage, and pay for all activities.

3. **Requesting an extension.** Easy BUD projects are hard-capped at 12 months. Do not write a project plan requiring 13+ months.

4. **Targeting an ineligible market.** The United States is not currently on the FTA/IPPA list. Verify before applying.

5. **Splitting one project across multiple Easy BUD applications.** HKPC treats this as circumventing the ceiling and will disqualify both.

6. **Budget items without 2 independent vendor quotations.** Every line item needs a minimum of 2 independent vendor quotes. A single quotation or the enterprise's own cost estimate is not acceptable.

7. **Applying within 3 months of a prior BUD submission.** The cooldown runs from the submission date regardless of outcome (approved, rejected, or withdrawn).

8. **Missing required documents.** Incomplete applications (no proof of HK operations, missing Business Registration, no vendor quotes) are returned without review.

## Document Checklist

### At Application Stage

- Business Registration Certificate (current and valid)
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Latest audited accounts (most recent financial year)
- Proof of substantive HK operations — one of: MPF contribution records for employees, or sales invoices/receipts
- Minimum **2 independent vendor quotations** for each budget line item
- Completed Probity / Non-Collusion Declaration (HKPC prescribed form, available at www.bud.hkpc.org)
- Completed Declaration on Concurrent BUD Funding (HKPC prescribed form)

### At Reimbursement Claim Stage (after project completion)

- Final Project Report (follow HKPC template)
- Final Audited Accounts prepared by an **independent external auditor**
- Invoices, receipts, and proof of payment for every eligible expenditure
- Delivery or completion evidence for each activity (screenshots, campaign reports, certificates, platform screenshots)
- Bank statements evidencing the enterprise's 75% cash contribution
- External auditor fee invoice (for Activity 4.10 if claimed)

**No reimbursement is released until both the Final Project Report and the Final Audited Accounts are formally accepted by the Programme Management Committee.**

---

## Cooldown Rule

An enterprise may submit **only one BUD application (of any track) every 3 months**.

- The restriction runs from the date of submission, regardless of outcome — approved, rejected, or withdrawn.
- Applies across all BUD tracks: an Easy BUD submission on January 15 means no BUD General submission until April 15.
- Having an approved active project running does not extend the cooldown — once 3 months have elapsed from the last submission, a new application can be submitted.

---

## Concurrent and Cumulative Caps

| Cap | Limit |
|---|---|
| Concurrent BUD funding (all on-going projects, any track) | HK$800,000 |
| Cumulative BUD funding (lifetime, any track) | HK$7,000,000 (max 70 approved projects) |
| Cumulative IP registration costs (Activity 4.3, any track, lifetime) | HK$600,000 |

The HK$800,000 concurrent cap is the maximum total approved outstanding funding across all currently active BUD projects. If you have a General Application approved at HK$600,000 still running, you may still apply for Easy BUD — but combined outstanding approved funding cannot exceed HK$800,000.

## Reimbursement Process (Step by Step)

1. **Application submitted** via HKPC online e-form at www.bud.hkpc.org.
2. **Application approved (~30 working days)** — HKPC issues Approval Letter with Offer Date. The 12-month project period starts from the Offer Date.
3. **Project execution** — enterprise carries out all approved activities within 12 months, paying all costs upfront.
4. **Project completion** — all activities completed; gather all invoices, receipts, and completion evidence.
5. **External audit** — engage an independent external auditor to produce Final Audited Accounts. Budget auditor fee under Activity 4.10 (up to HK$5,000).
6. **Reimbursement claim submission** — submit Final Project Report + Final Audited Accounts + all supporting documents to HKPC.
7. **PMC review** — Programme Management Committee reviews the claim; may request clarifications.
8. **Reimbursement paid** — HKPC disburses 25% of verified eligible costs to the enterprise's bank account.

## Easy BUD vs. BUD General Application

| Aspect | Easy BUD | General Application |
|---|---|---|
| Activity type | Type ii only (self-implemented specific measures) | Type i or Type ii |
| Project funding ceiling | **HK$150,000** | HK$800,000 |
| Project duration | 12 months max, no extensions | Up to 24 months |
| Initial payment | None (reimbursement only) | 20% upfront payment option available |
| Implementation | HK entity only; must be self-run | Can engage external service providers in target market |
| Processing | ~30 working days | ~60+ working days |
| Application complexity | Lower | Higher |
| Best for | First-time, focused, self-run market-entry | Large-scale expansion with external partners |

**Recommendation for first-time applicants:** Start with Easy BUD. The lower ceiling and 30-day processing let you get a first approval and reimbursement on record quickly, before tackling a larger General Application.
